The Internet

The internet, a vast global network of interconnected computers, has fundamentally transformed nearly every aspect of modern life. Born out of the ARPANET project in the late 1960s, it has grown exponentially to become an indispensable part of our daily existence. This summary is all about the key components, impact on society, and potential challenges.

The internet's development can be divided into several stages. It initially served as a means for academic and military institutions to share information. Tim Berners-Lee's invention of the World Wide Web in 1989, along with the introduction of web browsers, marked a turning point, enabling user-friendly access to information. Today, it encompasses billions of websites and connects over 4.9 billion people worldwide, fostering global communication and commerce.

Key Terms

Cloud computing: Internet-based computing that provides shared computer processing resources and data to computers and other devices on demand.

Communication protocol: A system of rules that allow two or more entities of a communications system to transmit information.

Computer security: The protection of computer systems from theft or damage to their hardware, software, or information, as well as from disruption or misdirection of the services they provide.
