Computer graphics, encompassing both pictures and films created through computational processes, have played a transformative role across various media forms, revolutionizing animation, movies, advertising, video games, and graphic design. One significant aspect of computer graphics is computer animation, the art of crafting moving images through the utilization of computers.

In the realm of computer graphics, two fundamental types are raster graphics (or bitmap images) and vector graphics. Raster graphics are represented as a dot matrix data structure, forming a rectangular grid of pixels. These images are resolution-dependent, meaning they cannot scale up without a loss of quality. On the other hand, vector graphics employ polygons to represent images, offering scalability without compromising quality. Vector art, ideal for printing due to its mathematical curves, ensures crisp printing even when resized.

Raster-based image editors, such as Painter, Photoshop, and GIMP, focus on pixel-level editing, while vector-based image editors like CorelDRAW and Inkscape center around editing lines and shapes (vectors). The HTML element facilitates the insertion of images into web pages. The src attribute specifies the image URL, and the alt attribute provides alternative text for cases where the image cannot be displayed.
